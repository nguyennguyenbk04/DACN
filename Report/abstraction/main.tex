\section*{\Huge Abstract}
Although online education has expanded rapidly, the ways learners interact with long-form video lectures remain inefficient and cognitively demanding. Students often face difficulty revisiting lengthy lecture recordings, especially when trying to identify key concepts or prepare for exams. Research from the Online Learning Consortium reports that 72\% of students struggle to revise effectively using long recorded lectures, frequently missing essential information during review sessions. At the same time, 67\% of university instructors (EDUCAUSE Review) believe that students would significantly benefit from structured summaries and interactive reinforcement tools.\\

Furthermore, findings from the Journal of Educational Technology show that students who study using summarized material and self-assessment quizzes experience a 25\% improvement in comprehension and retention compared to those relying solely on video content. These insights highlight the limitations of traditional lecture formats—passive, time-consuming, and not optimized for active learning or personalized review.\\

Beyond academic environments, the ability to condense, organize, and interact with spoken content is equally valuable in professional training programs, seminars, and corporate learning environments. Transforming lengthy spoken content into structured, interactive formats supports exam preparation, collaborative study, and the development of long-term knowledge.\\

In response to these challenges, \textbf{Quizum} is developed as a comprehensive solution that enhances lecture-based learning through AI-powered automation. By converting video lectures into summaries, quizzes, and structured study materials, \textbf{Quizum} improves accessibility, knowledge retention, and learner engagement across educational and training contexts.
